Enumerable#group_by

Technical Blog

May 4th, 2015

Ruby's Enumerable Module is another way of looping in your code.Enumerables are preferred over looping because it is easier to see what is going on.

Now, I will talk about the #group_by method. The #group_by method does just what it sounds like. It groups the data based on the block you input. The output is going to be a hash of strings, arrays or even numbers. Let's take a look at an example.

Let's say you have an array of numbers.

numbers = [1,2,3,4,5,6]

Now we are going to group these numbers using modulo (%). If you are unfamiliar with what a modulo is, you can think of it is the remainder. For example, when you do 6/3, there is no remainder. Therefore, 6%3 would be 0. However, 6/5 has a remainder of 1 so 6%5 would be 1. Now we are going to group the numbers array by using %. We are going to separate the numbers by the values of the modulo.

(1..6).group_by {|i| i%3}

So the numbers 1 through 6 are iterating through the loop and each number is getting modulo-ed by 3.

{0=>[3,6], 1=>[1,4], 2=>[2,5]}

As you can see, the #group_by method creates a hash that groups the number array based on the value of their modulo.
