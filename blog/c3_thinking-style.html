Thinking Style and Learning

4/25/2015

After taking the personal thinking style quiz, I realized that I have an equal makeup of pretty much all of the thinking styles. My results showed 28 for Concrete Sequential and Concrete Random and a 32 for Abstract Sequential and Abstract Random. This freaked me out a bit because I was worried this meant that I had no thinking style at all. However, for the purpose of finding out my thinking style to help myself learn better at DBC, I decided to concetrate on Abstract Sequential and Abstract Random styles. Upon doing on these two styles, I found it very funny that these are pretty much the opposite thinking style to have.

The Abstract Sequential thinkers learn best when they are able to work alone. Which is true. I tend to enjoy working alone because I believe that it saves up a lot of time. I also enjoy it when my points are heard. I like to know that my peers are listening to what I am saying and taking it seriously. However, the Abstract Random thinker likes to listen to others which is also true. I also like to hear what others have to say and I learn a lot from others' points of view. The research also stated that Abstract Random thinkers learn best in group activities. Upon having done 2 GPS sessions and 4 pair sessions already, I realize that I do enjoy working with other people when everyone is focused and collaborating. So after the research, I realized that I like to visualize the problem and experiment with the problem. Many times while doing the exercises, I found myself trying different methods out and running it then making changes from there. At DBC, I will keep my challenges hands-on and try to tackle them by experimenting and trying different things.

So far, the hardest part of DBC was the time management. I stressed the most on week 1 of phase 0 because I found out that we had challenges due Friday night. That was a mistake on my part for not reading the details carefully. However, starting the challenges that are meant to be done in a week was very stressfully for me when I tried to rush it in two days. I really rushed myself in the challenges so I missed a lot of the important details. When I moved onto the harder challenges, I found myself struggling and not knowing a lot of the information. I panicked and drew a conclusion that DBC was too hard for me. However, after taking an extension for the week 1, I was able to go through the material again and learn the materials I missed. Once I got that done, I started to really enjoy doing my work. As for the material, I am doing quite well in it. I will continue to keep track of my material and stay on top of it so that I don't miss materials. From now on, when I find myself struggling with the material, rather than thinking I am not good enough for DBC, I will try to have the growth mindset and see my obstacles as a way for growth.
